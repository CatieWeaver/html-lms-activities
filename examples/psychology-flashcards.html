Psychology Concept
Classical Conditioning
Click to reveal definition and examples

Definition: A learning process where a neutral stimulus becomes associated with a meaningful stimulus, producing a similar response.

Example: Pavlov's dogs learned to salivate at the sound of a bell after it was repeatedly paired with food presentation
Key Components: Unconditioned stimulus, conditioned stimulus, unconditioned response, conditioned response
